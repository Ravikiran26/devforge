## What DevForge Actually Is

DevForge is not a course platform. It is a **structured project-building program**.

Students don't watch lectures. They build real, production-grade software by following weekly project briefs, opening pull requests, and getting those PRs reviewed by AI. By the end of 8 weeks, they have a GitHub profile full of merged PRs across 3 real projects — the kind of work that answers "show me something you've built" in an interview.

The higher-tier plans layer career support on top of that: resume help, mock interviews (conducted by hired freelance developers), and a structured job application process.

## Plan 1 — Solo · ₹7,999

### Who is this for?
Self-driven students who want the full curriculum and AI feedback but don't need career support yet. They're focused on building skills and will figure out the job side on their own. Typically students who already have some coding experience and want a structured way to build real projects.

---

### What's Included

#### 1. 8-Week Project Curriculum (Self-Paced)
The core of the program. Eight weeks of structured project briefs, each building on the last.

**What you get each week:**
- A written lesson explaining the concept (what it is, why it matters, how it works)
- A project brief describing exactly what feature to build
- Code examples and reference material
- A GitHub workflow to follow (branch → build → PR)

**What you build across 8 weeks:**
- Week 1 — Git, Terminal, and setting up a full-stack development environment
- Week 2 — Express backend with routes, middleware, and Prisma ORM
- Week 3 — JWT authentication, refresh tokens, and role-based access control
- Week 4 — React frontend with state management, forms, and TanStack Query
- Week 5 — Payment integration using Razorpay (real payments, not sandbox-only)
- Week 6 — File uploads and storage using Cloudinary
- Week 7 — Real-time features using Socket.io
- Week 8 — Deployment to Vercel, environment configs, and CI/CD basics

By the end of Week 8, the student has 3 deployed, working applications on their GitHub.

#### 2. AI-Powered PR Reviews (Every Pull Request)
This is the most important feature of the platform.

Every time a student opens a pull request, it is automatically reviewed by Claude (Anthropic's AI model). The review is delivered as inline comments directly on the PR — exactly how code review works at a real company.

**What the AI reviews:**
- Is the logic correct? Does it do what it claims?
- Are variables, functions, and files named clearly?
- Are there security issues (e.g., unprotected routes, exposed secrets)?
- Is the code structured in a way that would survive a real codebase?
- Are there obvious improvements the student missed?

Students can fix the issues, push again, and get a fresh review. This loop — build → review → fix → re-review — is what creates real learning.

#### 3. Community Discord Access
Access to the DevForge Discord server, shared across all cohorts.

**What's available:**
- Per-week channels where students post progress and ask questions
- A general help channel for debugging and unblocking
- A showcase channel to share what you've built
- Peer support from students at the same stage and alumni from earlier cohorts

There is no guaranteed response from DevForge staff in this plan. Support is peer-driven.

#### 4. Completion Certificate
Issued after the student finishes all 8 weeks and merges a minimum of 20 pull requests.

The certificate includes the student's full name, program name, completion date, and a unique verification link. It can be added to LinkedIn under Licenses & Certifications.

#### 5. Lifetime Content Access
The student's account never expires. When curriculum is updated (new tools, updated project briefs, additional lessons), those changes are reflected in their account automatically at no extra cost.

- No mock interviews
- No LinkedIn profile support

---

- 2–3 focused hours per day
- No fixed schedule — fully self-paced
- Can be completed in 8 weeks or stretched to 16 weeks with no penalty

## Plan 2 — Cohort · ₹13,999

### Who is this for?
Students who are close to graduating or recently graduated and want to get placed within 2–3 months of finishing. They want the project experience AND structured help with their resume and job applications. They may have never written a technical resume before or don't know where to start with job applications in the developer market.

---

### What's Included
Everything in Solo, plus:

#### 1. Resume Writing Guidance
Most student resumes are either blank or full of college project descriptions that don't impress technical hiring managers. We fix this.

**What's provided:**
- A DevForge resume template designed for freshers and junior developers in India
- A written guide: what to include, what to cut, how to describe projects, and what keywords matter
- Examples of strong and weak project descriptions so students can rewrite their own
- A checklist to self-review before sending to companies

The student writes their own resume using this guidance. They leave with a resume that can get them past the first filter.

#### 2. Job Application Strategy
Most students apply randomly and hear nothing back. We provide a structured process.

**What's provided:**
- A list of company categories to target as a fresher (early-stage startups, product companies, service companies — ordered by likelihood of hire and quality of experience)
- A weekly application plan: how many to send, how to prioritise, how to follow up
- Cover letter template and guidance on when to use one vs when not to
- A job application tracker (spreadsheet) to monitor status and next steps
- Guidance on using LinkedIn, Naukri, and direct company career pages effectively

#### 3. Interview Preparation Resources
Basic interview prep material to understand what technical interviews for junior developer roles look like in India.

**What's provided:**
- A curated list of the most commonly asked DSA problems for fresher interviews (with solutions and explanations)
- System design questions that appear at the junior level — with example answers
- Guidance on how to do a project walkthrough in an interview ("walk me through something you built")
- Behavioral question prep: how to answer "tell me about yourself", "why do you want to join us", etc.

#### 4. 1 Mock Interview Session
One mock technical interview conducted by a freelance senior developer hired by DevForge.

**What happens:**
- 45-minute video call with an experienced developer (3+ years of industry experience)
- Format: 1 DSA problem + 1 project walkthrough of the student's own work + 2–3 behavioral questions
- After the session, written feedback is shared covering what went well and what to improve
- Feedback is specific — not generic ("your communication was good") but actionable ("when you explained the auth flow, you skipped why you chose JWT over sessions — practice explaining that decision")

The interviewer is a hired freelancer, not a DevForge employee. They are briefed on what to test and how to give feedback.

---

### What Cohort Does NOT Include
- No personal resume rewrite (student writes their own using our guidance)
- No dedicated 1:1 support (Discord is the support channel)
- No more than 1 mock interview
- No LinkedIn profile rewrite

---

### Time Commitment
- 3–4 hours per day recommended
- No fixed schedule — fully self-paced like Solo
- Mock interview is scheduled by the student at any point after Week 6

## Plan 3 — 1:1 Placement · ₹24,999

### Who is this for?
Students who want maximum support and the highest chance of getting placed quickly. They want someone to personally review and rewrite their resume, do multiple mock interviews to build confidence, and guide them through each step of the application process. They may struggle with self-presentation or have had failed interviews before and want to know exactly what to fix.

---

### What's Included
Everything in Cohort, plus:

#### 1. Personal Resume Rewrite
Instead of writing their own resume using a template, a DevForge team member personally rewrites the student's resume after reviewing their work.

**What happens:**
- Student fills out a brief intake form: their background, the projects they built, any prior experience
- A team member from DevForge writes a complete resume draft
- The student reviews and provides feedback
- One revision round is included
- Final resume is ATS-optimised (passes keyword scanning used by most hiring platforms) and written in language that technical hiring managers respond to

This is not editing — it's a full rewrite from scratch based on what the student actually built in the program.

#### 2. LinkedIn Profile Complete Overhaul
LinkedIn is where most Indian tech recruiters source junior candidates. A weak profile means no inbound reach-outs.

**What's done:**
- Headline rewritten to be role-specific and searchable (e.g., "Full-Stack Developer | Node.js · React · PostgreSQL · Open to Work")
- About section rewritten to tell the student's story in 3–4 clear sentences
- Experience / Projects section updated with DevForge projects described in recruiter-friendly language
- Guidance on profile photo, banner, and activity settings
- One revision round included

#### 3. 3 Mock Interview Sessions
Three full mock interview sessions, each conducted by a different freelance developer hired by DevForge.

**Why three?**
- First session: assess where the student is and what's weak
- Second session: after practice, test if the weak areas improved
- Third session: final confidence check before active applications begin

Each session follows the same format as the Cohort plan's mock interview. After each session, written feedback is provided. Students can request to repeat a specific format (e.g., "I only want DSA practice") if needed — within the 3-session allowance.

#### 4. Application Tracking & Guidance
The job application process in India is messy and slow. This provides structure.

**What's provided:**
- Weekly async check-in: student shares applications sent and responses received; DevForge team provides specific guidance on what to do next
- Help interpreting rejection or silence (is it the resume? the role level? the company stage?)
- Guidance on when to follow up and how
- Help deciding between offers if multiple come in

This is async — via Discord or email, not video calls. Response within 24 business hours.

- No support after 3 months post-program completion

- Mock interviews can be scheduled at any point after Week 5
- Application tracking begins after Week 8 (or earlier if the student is ready)

## Frequently Asked Questions (Sales & Onboarding)

**Q: Do you have live classes?**
No. DevForge is fully async. There are no scheduled live sessions. Students learn by building, not by watching.

**Q: Who conducts the mock interviews?**
Freelance developers with 3+ years of industry experience, hired and briefed by DevForge. They are not DevForge employees but follow a structured interview format and feedback rubric.

**Q: When should a student start job applications?**
After Week 6 at the earliest. Ideally after Week 8 when all 3 projects are deployed and on GitHub. Applying earlier with incomplete projects reduces the chance of interview calls.

**Q: What if a student doesn't get placed after 1:1 Mentored?**
We don't offer a placement guarantee. We offer structured support — strong projects, a good resume, and mock interview practice. Placement depends on the student's effort, communication, and the market. Most students who complete all 8 weeks and actively apply get responses within 4–6 weeks.

**Q: Can a student upgrade from Solo to Cohort later?**
Yes. They pay the difference (₹6,000) and get access to the additional features immediately.
